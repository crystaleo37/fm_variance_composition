\section{Study 2: CIFAR-10 Factorial Ablation}
\label{sec:study2}

\paragraph{Immediate observations.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item BatchOT is necessary but not sufficient: cell\_100 converged, but cells 101, 110, 111 did not.
    \item TPC causes NaN divergence in every configuration: cells 001, 101 both diverged (as did cell\_000, though for a different reason---see Section~\ref{sec:discussion}).
    \item StableVM causes wild oscillation in every configuration: cells 010, 011, 110, 111 all show severely unstable loss.
    \item The only successful combination is the simplest improved variant: BatchOT + standard CFM + Uniform sampling.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Training Loss Curves}

Figure~\ref{fig:training_curves} shows the training loss evolution for all 8 cells.

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/combined/combined_training_curves.pdf}
    \caption{Training loss curves for all 8 ablation cells. Green titles indicate convergence, red indicates NaN divergence, orange indicates unstable oscillation. Red dashed lines mark the step at which NaN first appeared.}
    \label{fig:training_curves}
\end{figure}

\textbf{Cell\_100} (BatchOT + CFM + Uniform) shows a smooth, monotonic decrease from 0.54 to 0.14 over 80k steps, characteristic of healthy flow-matching training.

\textbf{Cells 000, 001, 101} diverge to NaN at steps ${\sim}18{,}700$, ${\sim}16{,}600$, and ${\sim}14{,}400$ respectively. The pre-NaN loss is relatively low (0.15--0.25), suggesting that the model was learning before numerical instability triggered a cascade.

\textbf{Cells 010, 011, 110, 111} (all StableVM) exhibit extreme oscillation, with loss swinging between ${\sim}40$ and ${\sim}22{,}000$ throughout training. The model never stabilizes, despite 80k steps of training. This pattern is consistent across both coupling types and both estimators, pointing to StableVM as the root cause.

\subsection{Cell\_100: The Only Successful Model}

Since cell\_100 is the only cell that produced a meaningful generative model, we analyze it in detail.

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.7\textwidth]{figures/combined/cell_100_fid_detail.pdf}
    \caption{FID vs.\ NFE for cell\_100 (BatchOT + CFM + Uniform), the only converged cell. Three ODE solvers are compared.}
    \label{fig:cell100_fid}
\end{figure}

Table~\ref{tab:cell100_fid} shows the full FID results for cell\_100.

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\caption{FID values for cell\_100 across solvers and NFE. Best value in each column is bolded.}
\label{tab:cell100_fid}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lccccc@{}}
\toprule
Solver & NFE=5 & NFE=10 & NFE=20 & NFE=40 & NFE=100 \\
\midrule
Euler & 41.1 & 29.7 & 24.5 & 21.1 & 19.1 \\
Midpoint & \textbf{33.1} & \textbf{24.4} & \textbf{21.8} & \textbf{20.4} & 19.0 \\
RK4 & 180.7 & 104.6 & 44.3 & 23.0 & \textbf{18.4} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\paragraph{Solver behavior.} Midpoint achieves the best FID at low NFE (33.1 at NFE=5), outperforming Euler (41.1) by 19\%. RK4 struggles at very low NFE (180.7 at NFE=5) due to its high per-step cost---each ``step'' actually performs 4 function evaluations, so NFE=5 corresponds to barely one full integration step. At NFE=100, RK4 achieves the best FID of 18.4.

\paragraph{Practical recommendation.} For cell\_100, midpoint is the most efficient solver: it reaches FID~20.4 at only 40 NFE, matching Euler at 100 NFE. This 2.5$\times$ efficiency gain from solver choice is consistent with the trajectory-straightness findings from Study~1---BatchOT coupling produces paths that are well-suited to moderate-order solvers.

\subsection{Failed Cells: FID Context}

For completeness, Table~\ref{fig:fid_bars} reports the best FID achieved by each cell, even though only cell\_100 represents a meaningful generative model.

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.85\textwidth]{figures/combined/fid_comparison_bars.pdf}
    \caption{Best FID across all 8 cells. The stark contrast between cell\_100 (FID 18.4) and the rest (197--679) underscores the failure of the composition hypothesis.}
    \label{fig:fid_bars}
\end{figure}

The FID values of 679.2 for cells 000, 101 correspond to pure noise (the model at step 10k had barely begun learning when evaluated from a pre-NaN checkpoint). Cells 010--011 and 110--111 achieve FIDs in the 197--290 range, reflecting models that learned some coarse structure despite never stabilizing. Cell\_001 at 419.8 falls between, having learned slightly more than cells 000/101 before diverging.
