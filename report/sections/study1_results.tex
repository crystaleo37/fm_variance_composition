\section{Study 1: 2D Synthetic Experiments}
\label{sec:study1}

This section presents results from comparing four flow-matching path variants on 2D synthetic distributions. The goal is to build geometric intuition about how path design affects trajectory quality, solver robustness, and sample fidelity.

\subsection{NFE Robustness}

The low-budget regime is more discriminative than endpoint quality. Figure~\ref{fig:nfe_swd} shows SWD as a function of NFE for all four variants.

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.7\textwidth]{figures/study1/nfe_vs_swd.png}
    \caption{SWD vs.\ NFE on the checkerboard distribution. OT-CFM and SB-CFM maintain quality at low NFE, while VP-CFM degrades significantly below NFE=16.}
    \label{fig:nfe_swd}
\end{figure}

At low NFE ($\leq 8$), OT-CFM and SB-CFM achieve SWD around 0.022, while VP-CFM and Target-CFM reach approximately 0.042 and 0.039 respectively---roughly 47\% worse. As NFE increases, VP-CFM improves strongly, indicating high discretization sensitivity. OT-type paths are inherently more solver-robust because their straighter trajectories require fewer integration steps to approximate accurately.

\subsection{Trajectory Geometry}

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.7\textwidth]{figures/study1/curvature_vs_error.png}
    \caption{Trajectory curvature vs.\ endpoint error. OT-CFM and SB-CFM produce the straightest trajectories (curvature near 1.0), supporting their low-NFE robustness.}

OT-CFM and SB-CFM produce the straightest trajectories (mean curvature close to 1.0), while Target-CFM is substantially more curved. This directly explains the NFE robustness gap: straighter paths require fewer discretization steps to traverse accurately.

\paragraph{Time-to-structure.} An interesting nuance emerges from the time-to-structure analysis: Target-CFM achieves the best AUC on checkerboard (fastest early structural progress), despite not winning endpoint quality. This suggests that ``early geometric progress'' and ``final sample fidelity'' are related but not equivalent objectives.

\subsection{Key Takeaways from Study 1}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{SB-CFM wins endpoint quality} across all three datasets.
    \item \textbf{OT-CFM wins trajectory straightness and low-NFE robustness} on most datasets.
    \item Path design materially affects both sample quality and computational efficiency.
    \item All four variants work well in 2D---no instabilities or divergence observed.
\end{enumerate}

The last point will prove critical: the 2D setting masks numerical issues that become catastrophic in high dimensions (Section~\ref{sec:discussion}).
